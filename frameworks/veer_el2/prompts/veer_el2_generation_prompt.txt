================================================================================
EVALUATION (so you know what success looks like)
================================================================================
Your output will be assessed by the VeeR EL2 benchmark in `VerifEval`.
The pipeline runs the following checks against your candidate RTL, REUSING
the upstream golden testbench infrastructure:

    1. Module inventory match (vs golden)
    2. Top-module port match (names, directions)
    3. File-level diff similarity (proxy)
    4. Verible lint (verible-verilog-lint, all candidate files)
    5. Verilator --lint-only against design/flist.lint
    6. Verilator full build with the golden testbench (tb_top + test_tb_top.cpp)
    7. Smoke run of testbench/asm/hello_world (using precompiled hex)
    8. Vivado xelab elaboration

The single best signal of correctness is: passing the upstream `hello_world`
test under the unmodified upstream testbench.

================================================================================
WHAT YOU MUST NOT DO
================================================================================
* Do NOT modify the testbench, the Makefile, the lint flists, the configs,
  or anything outside `<run_dir>/candidate/{candidate_subdir}/`.
* Do NOT invent new top-level ports.
* Do NOT fetch the upstream repo or any extra context beyond the spec pack.
* Do NOT generate a verification environment / UVM / SVA — that is out of
  scope for this benchmark; testing is performed by the GOLDEN testbench
  against your generated RTL.

When you are done, ensure that the file `el2_veer_wrapper.sv` (or the
`{top_file}` listed above) exists in your candidate directory and that its
top module is named exactly `{top_module}` with the documented ports.
